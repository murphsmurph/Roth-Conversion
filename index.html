How it works: Each year, inflation-adjusted living expenses are funded from Social Security and other income, then RMDs, then by drawing accounts taxable → traditional → Roth. Federal and state tax are computed with true progressive brackets on each year's taxable income (not a flat rate); qualified dividends and long-term gains stack on top of ordinary income at 0/15/20% rates, and the 3.8% NIIT applies above the statutory MAGI thresholds ($200k single / $250k MFJ, unindexed). QCDs (70½+, 2026 limit $111,000/person) go straight from IRA to charity — excluded from income and MAGI, satisfying the RMD first. The conversion's tax cost is the exact marginal difference between the year's tax with and without the conversion — so torpedo, bracket-shift, and NIIT effects are all captured in it. RMDs use IRS Uniform Lifetime divisors per spouse. Social Security taxation uses the provisional-income formula (50%/85% tiers). At first death the household switches to Single brackets and the survivor keeps the larger benefit (the "widow's penalty"). IRMAA applies the 2026 surcharge with a 2-year MAGI lookback. Legacy is valued after-tax: Roth tax-free; remaining traditional reduced by the inheriting party's rate (SECURE Act 10-year drain for children).

Assumptions & limitations

Federal brackets, standard deduction, LTCG thresholds, SS thresholds, IRMAA tiers, and the QCD limit are 2026 figures held flat (not indexed forward). State tax uses each state's structure — no-tax (9 states), full retirement-income exemption (IL, MS, PA, IA), flat-rate, or graduated brackets — with simplified retirement-income treatment; graduated thresholds approximate single-filer schedules doubled for joint. Dividends are assumed qualified at a 2% yield; gains realized mid-year are taxed at that year's marginal stacked LTCG rate (bracket-boundary effects approximated). QCD eligibility (70½) applies from the year a spouse turns 71. Monte Carlo draws normally distributed annual returns (no fat tails or autocorrelation) and runs the identical market path through both plans. Roth five-year clocks are not enforced. RMD withdrawals are drawn pro-rata across spouses. This is a directional planning illustration, not tax preparation — confirm against the client's 1040, state return, and Form 8606 with a qualified professional.
